About This Tool

Compress your PDF online, completely free, without uploading it anywhere. This browser-based PDF compressor reduces file size fast by re-encoding page images at your chosen quality level, so you can email, upload, or share your document more easily. Because everything runs locally in your browser, your file stays private and secure the entire time — nothing is sent to a server. It works smoothly on mobile and desktop alike, including Android, iPhone, Windows, and Mac, in Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari. Best results come from image-heavy or scanned PDFs; text-based PDFs are often already compact. No signup, no watermark, no limits — just a fast, private way to shrink your PDF.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I compress a PDF online for free?

Choose your PDF file above, pick a compression level, and tap Compress PDF. The smaller file downloads directly to your device — no account or payment needed.

Is this PDF compressor really private?

Yes. Compression runs entirely inside your browser using JavaScript. Your file is never uploaded to a server, so no one else ever sees it.

Can I compress a PDF without uploading it?

Yes. That's exactly how this tool works — your PDF stays on your device the entire time.

How do I reduce PDF size while keeping quality?

Choose the "Low" compression level, which applies the lightest re-encoding and best preserves image quality while still reducing file size.

Can I compress a PDF on Android?

Yes. This tool works in any modern mobile browser on Android, including Chrome and Firefox.

Can I compress a PDF on iPhone?

Yes. It works in Safari and Chrome on iPhone and iPad without needing to install an app.

What browsers are supported?

Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari on both desktop and mobile are all supported.

Does this work on Windows and Mac?

Yes. Since it runs in your browser, it works the same way on Windows, Mac, and Linux.

There's no fixed limit — the practical limit depends on your device's available memory, since everything is processed locally.

Is this the best free PDF compressor?
