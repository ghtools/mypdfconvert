Frequently Asked Questions

Which compression level should I choose?

"Medium" works well for most files. Choose "Low" if you need to preserve image quality, or "High" if file size matters more than quality — for example, when emailing a large scanned document.

Why didn't my PDF get much smaller?

Text-based PDFs (like Word exports) are often already small and compact, so compression has less to work with. Compression makes the biggest difference on image-heavy or scanned PDFs.

Will compressing reduce the readability of text?

Compression re-renders each page as an image at your chosen quality level, so very small text may look slightly softer at high compression. Choose "Low" compression if text clarity matters most.

Is my PDF uploaded to compress it?

No. Compression happens entirely in your browser — your file is never uploaded to a server.
